# IBM MQ Bootcamp

## Module 02 - Administration & Operations

Enterprise Administration for IBM MQ Environments

---

## Overview

This module introduces the operational and administrative responsibilities required to manage IBM MQ environments in production.

Students learn how to administer Queue Managers, manage MQ objects, configure communication components, monitor environments and implement operational best practices used by enterprise organizations.

The content is designed for administrators, middleware engineers, support teams and integration specialists responsible for operating IBM MQ infrastructures.

## Module Contents

### Chapter 01 - IBM MQ Administration Overview

Introduction to IBM MQ administration, operational responsibilities and enterprise environments.

### Chapter 02 - Queue Manager Lifecycle

Queue Manager architecture, startup and shutdown procedures, recovery concepts and operational best practices.

### Chapter 03 - MQSC Fundamentals

MQSC command language, command structure, scripting, automation and configuration management.

### Chapter 04 - Queue Administration

Queue types, attributes, monitoring, capacity management and operational best practices.

### Chapter 05 - Channel Administration

Channel architecture, communication flows, channel states, troubleshooting and administration.

### Chapter 06 - Listener Administration

TCP/IP connectivity, listener management, connectivity validation and troubleshooting.

### Chapter 07 - Service Objects

Service definitions, automation and background process management.

### Chapter 08 - Monitoring Fundamentals

Monitoring strategies, queue depth analysis, channel monitoring, FDC files and operational visibility.

### Chapter 09 - Backup and Configuration Management

Configuration backup, saveqmgr, dmpmqcfg, recovery concepts and change management.

### Chapter 10 - Common Operational Tasks

Daily administration activities and operational procedures.

### Chapter 11 - Operational Best Practices

Naming standards, environment separation, monitoring and documentation.

### Chapter 12 - Module Summary

Review of key concepts and preparation for subsequent modules.
